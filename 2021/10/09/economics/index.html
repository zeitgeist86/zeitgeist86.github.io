Notes on Topics of Economics

发表于 2021-10-09

This is the notes on my graduate course Topics of Economics, which basicallly covers asset pricing (from a financial ecomonics point of view) and macroeconomics analysis.

1. Risk Premium Puzzle

The equity risk premium puzzle states that, equity are "overvalued" with respect to bonds

1.1 Stochastic Discount Factor

  • Stochastic discount factor (SDF) : Consider a market with money market account \(R_t\) and \(i\) divident-paying assets \(S_{i,t}\), then the random variable \(\Lambda\) is a SDF if
    • \(\Lambda_0=1\)
    • \(\forall t, Pr(\Lambda_t>0)=1\)
    • \(\Lambda_t\times S_t\) and \(\Lambda_t\times R_t\) are martingales
  • For the last property, \(\forall T>t\), we have: \(\Lambda_t\times S_{i,t}=E(\Lambda_T\times S_{i,T}|\mathcal{F}_t)=E_t(\Lambda_T\times S_{i,T})\), so \(E_t[\Lambda_T/\Lambda_t S_{i,t}]=S_{i,t}\)
  • More generally, we have \[P_t=E_t\left[\int_{s=0}^{\infty} \frac{\Lambda_{t+s}}{\Lambda_t}D_{t+s}ds\right]\] where
    • \(D_t\): the dividend yielded by the asset
    • \(P_t\): price of the asset

1.2 Processes

  • \(\Lambda_t\) is a decreasing function of \(t\) but is always positive, so we can write its process as: \[\frac{d\Lambda_t}{\Lambda_t}=-r_t^fdt-\sigma_t^\Lambda dZ_t\] where
    • \(r_t^f\): risk-free rate
    • \(\sigma_t^\Lambda\): market price of risk
  • For ex-divident price of an asset \(P_t\), we have \[\frac{dP_t}{P_t}=\mu_t^Pdt+\sigma_t^PdZ_t\] where
    • \(\mu_t^PP_t\): instantaneous expected capital gain
    • \(\sigma_t^PP_t\): exposure of the asset to the shock \(Z_t\)
  • The instantaneous return \(R_t\) is the sum of the return of the capital gains and the return of dividents, so \[dR_t=\frac{dP_t+D_tdt}{P_t}\]

1.3 Basic Asset Pricing models

  • By no arbitrage condition, the one-period pricing equation is \[-\Lambda_tD_tdt=E_t[d(\Lambda_tP_t)]\]

  • Using Itô's lemma, we have \(d(\Lambda_tP_t)=P_td\Lambda_t+\Lambda_tdP_t+dP_td\Lambda_t\). Rewrite the one-period pricing euqation, we get \[\frac{D_t}{P_t}+\mu_t^P-r^f_t=\sigma_t^P\sigma_t^\Lambda\] where left hand side is the excess return, and the right hand side is the covariance between returns and (minus) change in subjective valuation.

1.4 Usage

  • Use price \(P_t\) data to estimate SDF \(\Lambda_t\)
  • Model a SDF \(\Lambda_t\) to compute the correspoding price \(P_t\)
  • We will focus on the second approach
